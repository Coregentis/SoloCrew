# SOLOCREW Secretary To Cell Handoff Contract v0.1

## Purpose

This document freezes how `v0.5-portfolio-secretary-beta` may hand off bounded intent, management, or inspection context from the Secretary beta layer to a cell.

It exists to preserve:

- cell autonomy at product level
- explicit upstream runtime ownership
- explicit non-promotion relative to MPLP

## Handoff Goal

The Secretary-to-cell handoff contract must allow the Secretary beta layer to pass bounded context into a selected cell without collapsing:

- cell-local product identity into Secretary law
- runtime-private truth into product law
- product-layer semantics into protocol law

## Lawful Handoff Content

A lawful handoff may include bounded product-facing context such as:

- selected cell reference
- bounded management intent
- bounded queue or review posture
- bounded delivery-return context
- bounded approval or escalation context
- bounded continuity or inspection context

## What The Handoff Is Not

The handoff is not:

- direct runtime mutation authority
- direct provider execution authority
- direct channel authority
- protocol-law evidence of new envelope adoption
- a collapse of Secretary and cell into one role

## Ownership Boundary

The Secretary beta layer owns:

- product-level initiation and handoff packaging
- product-level visibility and coordination posture

The cell layer owns:

- cell-local product projection and interpretation
- bounded cell-local management and inspection semantics

`Cognitive_OS` owns:

- runtime-private truth
- runtime-private object identity
- runtime-private machine-readable authority

MPLP owns:

- frozen protocol core only

## Required Boundary Statements

Every lawful Secretary-to-cell handoff in `v0.5` must preserve that:

- the handoff is product-projected
- the handoff is bounded
- the handoff does not itself become runtime law
- the handoff does not itself become protocol law
- the selected cell remains a distinct product surface

## Deferred Beyond This Contract

This contract does not include:

- full execution routing
- full dispatch workflow
- automatic approval engine behavior
- direct delivery-return mutation
- cross-channel dispatch
- enterprise queue governance

## Contract Rule

If a proposed handoff feature mainly turns the Secretary into:

- runtime owner
- cross-cell executor
- direct approval engine
- protocol-level coordinator

then it exceeds this contract.

If it lets the Secretary beta layer initiate bounded product handoff into a selected cell while preserving explicit ownership boundaries, it fits this contract.
